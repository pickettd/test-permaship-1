## Overview

The Create Community App pilot at Center serves four primary user personas, each with distinct roles, needs, and interaction patterns within the platform. A fifth optional persona covers external facilitators.

---

## Persona 1: Kamaliʻi (Youth Participant)

**Who they are:**  
Youth participants enrolled in Center programs, ages 10–18. Drawn from multiple programs and selected based on active enrollment, ʻohana consent, and willingness to engage in digital literacy activities.

**Pilot target:** 20–30 participants

**Goals:**
- Participate in group discussions, respond to prompts, and reflect on experiences
- Engage with cultural and educational content (moʻolelo, oli, cultural challenges)
- Build digital literacy and practice respectful online behavior
- Earn recognition through gamification features (badges, points) in the trial subgroup

**Key app interactions:**
- Posting to group feeds and responding to staff prompts
- Viewing announcements and program updates
- Reporting inappropriate or concerning content
- Participating in gamification activities (optional subgroup)

**Constraints & considerations:**
- All posts require Admin approval before visibility
- App usage restricted to 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM
- Must complete a digital literacy overview before receiving posting privileges
- Requires ʻohana consent for digital engagement
- Varying comfort levels with technology and devices

**Success indicators:**
- ≥ 60% post, comment, or react at least once per week
- ≥ 85% report a positive or very positive experience
- Demonstrates understanding of digital safety and respectful dialogue

## Persona 2: ʻOhana (Parent/Caregiver) — Phase 2

**Who they are:**  
Parents and caregivers of enrolled kamaliʻi. Introduced in Phase 2 of the pilot to receive communications and provide feedback on the app experience.

**Pilot target:** 20–25 participants

**Goals:**
- Stay informed about program updates, events, and reminders from staff
- Understand and trust how their child is engaging digitally
- Provide feedback on the platform's usefulness and safety

**Key app interactions:**
- Receiving one-way communications and announcements from staff
- Accessing program updates and event reminders
- Completing optional app-based surveys or discussion participation
- Reviewing and consenting to their child's digital participation

**Constraints & considerations:**
- Must sign a digital communications consent form before access
- Requires onboarding session covering app navigation, privacy, and data use
- Varying familiarity with technology; user guide provided (print or digital)
- Privacy and data use are primary concerns

**Success indicators:**
- ≥ 90% of invited ʻohana complete onboarding
- Positive feedback on communication clarity and ease of use
- Report feeling informed about their child's program participation

## Persona 3: App Admin (Staff — Moderation Role)

**Who they are:**  
Designated Center staff responsible for content moderation, safety enforcement, and daily platform oversight. Drawn from the broader staff pool with elevated platform permissions.

**Pilot target:** Subset of the 5–10 staff participants

**Goals:**
- Maintain a safe and culturally appropriate digital environment for all users
- Approve content before it becomes visible to participants
- Respond quickly to flagged content, user reports, and safety incidents
- Track platform activity and contribute to pilot data collection

**Key app interactions:**
- Reviewing and approving all submitted posts within 24 hours
- Responding to automated keyword flag alerts
- Processing user-submitted reports
- Completing daily monitoring logs (activity, flags, incidents, analytics)
- Scheduling content and announcements (including during off-hours)

**Constraints & considerations:**
- Must complete Admin training before launch (post approval workflow, incident response, moderation protocols)
- Expected to log in and review activity daily
- Responsible for following Whole-Company's Incident Response Protocol for safety events (document, notify, escalate within 24 hours)
- Use a standard content checklist for moderation decisions

**Success indicators:**
- 100% of flagged content reviewed and resolved within 24 hours
- Consistent daily monitoring log completion
- Timely incident response with documented follow-up

## Persona 4: Center Staff (Content Contributor / Pilot Observer)

**Who they are:**  
Program Leads, Facilitators, Engagement Team members, and Operations staff who use the app to support program delivery, communicate with participants, and capture pilot learnings.

**Pilot target:** 5–10 staff participants (includes Admins)

**Sub-roles:**
- **Program Leads/Facilitators** — Select participants, support onboarding, monitor engagement, post program content
- **Engagement Team** — Reinforce safe app use with families, collect survey feedback
- **Operations Team** — Support logistics and implementation oversight
- **Implementation Team** — Provide tech support, document issues, analyze pilot data, conduct weekly check-ins

**Goals:**
- Communicate program updates, schedules, and cultural content to participants
- Support kamaliʻi engagement and digital literacy development
- Document observations, usability feedback, and technical issues
- Contribute to mid-point and final evaluation activities

**Key app interactions:**
- Posting announcements, program updates, and cultural content
- Sending direct messages or group-based updates to ʻohana
- Conducting weekly virtual walk-throughs and implementation check-ins
- Administering surveys and focus groups at Week 6 and Week 12
- Maintaining pilot activity logs and contributing to the evaluation report

**Constraints & considerations:**
- Must complete Staff Orientation before launch (roles, digital safety protocols, cultural integrity guidelines)
- Cultural staff are additionally responsible for reviewing content for protocol, language (ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi), and values alignment
- Feedback informs configuration changes and the final rollout recommendation

**Success indicators:**
- Staff affirm the app supports cultural content delivery without duplicating or complicating existing workflows
- Admin feedback confirms the system can scale with adjustments
- Consistent participation in weekly check-ins and debrief sessions

---

## Persona 5: External Facilitator / Pilot Partner (Optional)

**Who they are:**  
Contractors or third-party providers involved in program delivery (e.g., teaching artists). May be granted temporary access during the pilot to test third-party communication protocols.

**Goals:**
- Communicate with participants and staff in the context of their program role
- Access relevant program content and updates

**Key app interactions:**
- Limited access based on assigned permissions
- Communication within designated program groups

**Constraints & considerations:**
- Access is temporary and granted only with appropriate permissions and guidelines
- Subject to the same content and safety protocols as staff
- Participation is optional and dependent on program need
