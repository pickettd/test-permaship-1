**Pilot Plan**  

**Create Community App at Center** 

**1.0 OBJECTIVES** 

The pilot implementation of the Create Community app at Center is designed  to test, evaluate, and refine how Whole-Company can responsibly and effectively use digital platforms to  enhance communication, strengthen engagement, and uphold cultural values in virtual  environments. The pilot will serve as a proof of concept and learning opportunity to guide a  broader whole-company-wide rollout. 

This pilot will focus on the following core objectives: 

**1.1 Evaluate Communication Effectiveness** 

• Assess the app’s ability to support real-time, accessible, and culturally  grounded communication between staff, kamaliʻi, and ʻohana. 

• Determine whether the app helps reduce communication gaps that often occur  in hybrid or group-based programming. 

• Identify which features (e.g., direct messages, posts, announcements) are most  useful for different user types. 

**1.2 Test Safety Protocols and Content Moderation** 

• Ensure that digital safety policies—such as post approval, keyword flagging, and  incident response—are functioning as intended. 

• Monitor for any instances of inappropriate content or behavior and assess the  responsiveness of Admins. 

• Validate the incident reporting workflow and escalation procedures across roles  (e.g., Admins, facilitators, safety leads).  
**1.3 Measure User Adoption, Engagement & Digital Literacy** 

• Track user onboarding and active engagement metrics, including login  frequency, message posting, comment participation, and use of gamification  tools. 

• Assess how well kamaliʻi understand and apply digital literacy principles (safe  posting, respectful dialogue, reporting tools). 

• Identify barriers to app use, such as access to devices, comfort with technology,  or unclear expectations. 

**1.4 Assess Cultural Relevance and Programmatic Alignment** 

• Determine whether the platform supports the delivery of cultural content (e.g.,  moʻolelo, oli, protocol) and encourages culturally grounded peer interaction in  alignment with Whole-Company values. 

• Evaluate if the app complements existing Whole-Company programs (e.g., Life Readiness,  Arts, Culture) without duplicating or complicating workflows. 

• Gather staff feedback on how the app integrates into program delivery and  whether it enhances or hinders their engagement strategies. 

**1.5 Identify Opportunities for Improvement and Scalability** 

• Surface usability issues, gaps in training, or missing functionality to be  addressed before expanding to other Sites. 

• Document best practices, key risks, and readiness indicators that can inform  the whole-company-wide rollout plan. 

• Test a limited gamification feature set to determine whether incentives  effectively increase sustained engagement. 

**1.6 Build Foundations for Future System Integration** 

• Assess whether key data collected in the app (e.g., participation, feedback,  interaction) can be meaningfully aligned with Whole-Company’s outcomes and reporting  frameworks. 

• Begin initial technical conversations about API readiness and the potential for  future integration with Whole-Company information & constituent management system. 

• Determine administrative requirements and governance structures needed to  maintain system integrity during long-term use.

**1.7 Strengthen ʻOhana Engagement and Trust in Digital Tools (Phase 2\)** 

• Gauge how ʻohana members interact with the app and whether it improves their  awareness of and participation in Whole-Company offerings. 

• Understand ʻohana concerns, preferences, and expectations related to privacy,  content, and access. 

• Use feedback to shape future ʻohana-facing digital strategies and onboarding  processes. 

**2.0 SCOPE** 

The pilot phase will be intentionally scoped to allow for focused testing, hands-on support,  and meaningful feedback from a diverse yet manageable group of users. This will allow the Center team to evaluate both the **functional** and **cultural** fit of the Create  Community app before proceeding to broader implementation across Whole Company. 

**2.1 Location & Duration** 

• **Primary Site:** Center, located in Hawaii

• **Pilot Duration:** 6 months (June 2025 – November 2025\) 

• **Pilot Timeline Milestones:** 

o **June 2025:** Pre-launch admin training, content development, and app  configuration. Launch orientation, onboarding, and initial rollout to  

selected user groups 

o **August 2025:** Mid-point evaluation and refinements. Close summer  programming 

o **September 2025:** Fall programs begin 

o **November 2025:** Final month of user engagement and data collection **2.2 Participants** 

The pilot will include a cross-section of the Center user community to test different  engagement styles, developmental stages, and communication needs. 

• **2.2.1 Kamaliʻi Participants** 

• Target: 20–30 youth participants 

• Age range: 10–18 years 

• Drawn from multiple programs  

• Selection criteria: 

o Enrolled in a program active during the pilot window  
o ʻOhana consent for digital engagement 

o Willingness to participate in digital literacy activities 

• **2.2.2 ʻOhana Participants (Phase 2\)** 

• Target: 20–25 ʻohana members (parents/caregivers of enrolled kamaliʻi) • Will be onboarded to receive: 

o Program updates 

o Event reminders 

o Communication from staff 

o Optional participation in app-based surveys or discussions 

• **2.2.3 Whole-Company Staff Participants** 

• 5–10 Center teammates including: 

o Program Leads/Facilitators 

o Engagement Team 

o Operations Team 

o Implementation Team 

• Roles will include: 

o App Admins (content approval, moderation) 

o Content Contributors (e.g., program posts, center  

announcements) 

o Pilot Observers (support staff capturing learnings and insights) 

• **2.2.4 Optional: Pilot Partners** 

• Contractors or external providers who are part of program delivery (e.g.,  teaching artists) may be granted temporary access with appropriate  permissions and guidelines, allowing testing of third-party  

communication protocols. 

**2.3 Functional Scope** 

The following features of the app will be tested during the pilot:

| Feature Pilot Use Case |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Announcements**  | Staff share program updates, schedule changes, and  reminders |
| **Group Posts**  | Kamaliʻi respond to prompts, participate in discussions, or  reflect on experiences, post assignments |

| Direct Messaging   (limited & safe) | Staff send one-way messages to ʻohana or group-based  updates |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Media Sharing**  | Program videos/highlights, images from activities, and  digital learning content |
| **Content Moderation  Tools** | Admin approval of all posts, real-time content flagging, and  incident response |
| **User Reporting**  | Kamaliʻi and ʻohana report inappropriate or concerning  content |
| **Gamification (optional  module)** | Badges for participation, points for respectful engagement |
| **Feedback Tools**  | App-based surveys or polls to assess user satisfaction and  gather ideas |

**2.4 App Configuration for the Pilot** 

• **Private Instance:** Access limited to invited participants only; not searchable or  downloadable by the general public 

• **Custom Branding:** Whole-Company and Center visuals and messaging will be integrated into the  app interface 

• **Moderation Settings:** All posts require admin approval; system will flag high risk keywords; flag reports will route to designated Admins 

• **Usage Window:** App access restricted to appropriate times of day (e.g., 7:00 AM  – 8:00 PM) to support healthy usage habits 

**2.5 Boundaries and Exclusions** 

To keep the scope focused and manageable, the pilot will **not** include: 

• Full-scale rollout to all Sites or Whole-Company sites 

• Complex system integrations with Whole-Company information & constituent management system 

• High-volume social or event-based campaigns 

• External marketing or outreach 

• ʻOhana participation/communications (will address in a later phase) 

This bounded approach ensures that the pilot remains focused on controlled testing,  safety, and meaningful learning before scaling across the whole-company.  
**3.0 PILOT TEST CONDITIONS** 

The following test conditions will be implemented during the pilot to ensure safety,  functionality, alignment, and the quality of data collected. These conditions will serve as a  framework to evaluate the app’s ability to meet the needs of kamaliʻi, ʻohana, and staff  while adhering to Whole-Company’s safety and communication standards. 

Each condition below reflects a specific **feature or protocol** that will be **enabled,  required, or monitored** throughout the pilot period. 

**3.1 Closed Access Environment** 

• The pilot app environment will be **invite-only** and **not discoverable** in public  app stores. 

• Access will be limited to: 

o Pre-registered kamaliʻi and ʻohana 

o Center staff designated for the pilot 

o Approved external facilitators (if applicable) 

• All users will receive **access codes or unique invite links** during onboarding. • Public sharing of download instructions will be prohibited. 

**3.2 Structured Onboarding Requirement** 

• All participants (kamaliʻi, ʻohana, and staff) must complete a **brief onboarding  session** that includes: 

o App navigation 

o Digital behavior expectations 

o How to report concerns 

o Privacy and data use policies 

• ʻOhana must sign a **digital communications consent form** and be provided  with a user guide (print or digital). 

• Kamaliʻi must participate in a short **digital literacy overview** before receiving  posting privileges.  
**3.3 Mandatory Admin Review of All Posts** 

• No content posted by kamaliʻi, ʻohana, or staff (outside of assigned Admins) will  be visible until **approved by an App Admin**. 

• Admins will receive real-time alerts and are expected to review submissions  within **24 hours**. 

• A standard content checklist (e.g., language, tone, safety, relevance, cultural  appropriateness) will be used for moderation. 

• Posts that violate guidelines will be archived and used for staff review, training,  or debrief. 

**3.4 Automated Flagging of Risk-Based Content** 

• The app will be configured with a **custom keyword filter** designed to flag high risk terms related to: 

o Bullying, threats, and harassment 

o Explicit or suggestive language 

o Mental health concerns (e.g., “I want to hurt myself”) 

o Violations of Whole-Company’s cultural standards or behavior protocols 

• Flagged content will trigger **immediate alerts** to designated Admins and will not  be published until reviewed. 

• Keywords will be reviewed and refined mid-pilot for accuracy and sensitivity. 

**3.5 Daily App Activity Monitoring** 

• Admins must log in and review app activity **daily**, including: 

o New posts and comments 

o Flagged content 

o User reports 

o System analytics 

• A **daily monitoring log** will be completed to track Admin actions and  observations, including flagged issues, response times, and usage trends.  
**3.6 Incident Response Protocol Activation** 

• Any instance of inappropriate content, concerning behavior, or safety risk will  follow Whole-Company’s **Incident Response Protocol**: 

o Document and preserve the content (e.g., screenshots) 

o Notify site leadership and the Safety Officer 

o Engage ʻohana when appropriate 

o Refer to Whole-Company’s mandated reporting process if applicable 

• A response must occur **within 24 hours**, and a follow-up plan will be logged and  reviewed weekly during the pilot. 

**3.7 Gamification Feature Trial (Optional Subgroup)** 

• One group of kamaliʻi will participate in a **controlled trial** of the app’s  gamification features: 

o Digital badges 

o Point accumulation for engagement 

o Recognitions for respectful participation 

• The goal is to assess whether gamification increases: 

o App usage frequency 

o Positive peer engagement 

o Motivation to complete cultural or educational challenges 

**3.8 Time-of-Day Posting Restrictions** 

• The app will restrict user-generated posts and messaging during designated off hours (e.g., 8:00 PM to 7:00 AM). 

• This encourages healthy screen time habits and mirrors AAP and Common  Sense Media recommendations for youth technology use. 

• Admins will still have the ability to schedule content or send morning reminders  during off-hours. 

**3.9 Data Tracking & Evaluation-Ready Configuration**  
• The app will be configured to collect meaningful data during the pilot, including: o Number of users onboarded 

o Frequency of use by role (kamaliʻi, ʻohana, staff) 

o Types of content posted (announcements, discussion prompts,  

responses) 

o Number of flags, reports, and administrative actions taken 

• Data will be compiled weekly for internal review and shared in aggregate in the  final pilot evaluation report. 

**3.10 Cultural Integrity Checkpoints** 

• Admins and cultural staff will review posted content for alignment with Whole-Company’s  values and cultural framework. 

• Cultural specialists may be consulted to help review or co-create content that  supports respectful use of ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi, moʻolelo, or oli. 

• Weekly debriefs with program staff will ensure that cultural intent is preserved,  and adjustments will be made as needed.

**5.0 DATA COLLECTION & EVALUATION** 

The Create Community app pilot at Center will include a data collection and evaluation  process to assess its effectiveness, safety, usability, cultural alignment, and scalability.  The purpose of this evaluation is to generate clear, actionable insights to inform decision making around a whole-company-wide rollout, ensuring that the app meets the diverse needs of  kamaliʻi, ʻohana, and staff while aligning with Whole-Company’s values and strategic goals.  
The evaluation will combine **quantitative metrics**, **qualitative feedback**, and  **observational data** across multiple user groups and roles. 

**5.1 Evaluation Framework** 

The evaluation will assess the pilot across five key domains: 

| Domain Evaluation Focus |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **User Engagement**  | How frequently and meaningfully users interact with the app |
| **Digital Safety**  | Effectiveness of safety features (e.g., moderation, flagging,  reporting) |
| **Cultural Relevance**  | How well the app supports cultural content, values, and Whole-Company’s  voice |
| **Program Integration**  | How well the app complements and enhances Whole-Company program  delivery |
| **Scalability &   Sustainability** | Administrative effort, system configuration, and readiness for  whole-company-wide adoption |

**5.2 Data Collection Methods** 

• **5.2.1 Quantitative Metrics (System-Generated Analytics)** 

Collected directly from the app dashboard or analytics tool: 

• Number of users onboarded (by type: kamaliʻi, ʻohana, staff) 

• Daily/weekly active users 

• Number of posts, comments, and reactions 

• Frequency of logins per user type 

• Time spent in the app 

• Number of posts flagged (by system or user) 

• Number of reported incidents and resolution time 

• Gamification activity (e.g., badges earned, points accumulated) 

These metrics will be monitored **weekly** by the Implementation Team and  reviewed during mid- and end-pilot check-ins. 

• **5.2.2 Qualitative Feedback** 

Collected through structured conversations and surveys: 

• **Kamaliʻi Surveys/Focus Groups** 

o What they like/don’t like about the app 

o Whether they feel safe and heard online  
o What they’ve learned about digital behavior 

• **ʻOhana Surveys/Feedback Sessions** 

o Ease of use and onboarding experience 

o Helpfulness of communication from staff 

o Perceived impact on their child’s engagement 

• **Staff Debriefs** 

o App usefulness in communicating with families 

o Content moderation experience 

o Cultural fit and programming integration 

o Suggestions for rollout improvements 

Each group will be surveyed **at the midpoint** (Week 6\) and **end of the pilot** (Week 21–22). 

• **5.2.3 Observation & Documentation** 

• App Admins and facilitators will maintain a **pilot activity log** that  includes: 

o Any technical issues or usability challenges 

o Feedback received during day-to-day use 

o Notes from incidents or unusual user behavior 

• Implementation leads will conduct **weekly observations** and virtual  walk-throughs with Admins and participants. 

**5.3 Evaluation Success Criteria**

| Category Success Indicator |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Adoption**  | ≥ 90% of *invited* users (kamaliʻi and ʻohana) complete onboarding (not all users invited will opt in \- assuming a 10% opt out rate) |
| **Engagement**  | ≥ 60% of users post, comment, or react at least once a week |
| **Safety**  | 100% of flagged content reviewed and resolved within 24 hours |
| **User   Satisfaction** | ≥ 85% of respondents report a positive or very positive experience |
| **Cultural Fit**  | Cultural staff affirm app supports protocol, language, and values  appropriately |
| **Scalability   Readiness** | Admin feedback indicates system can be managed at larger scale  with adjustments |

**6.0 DATA PRIVACY & ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS** 

• No personal health information (PHI) or case-sensitive data will be collected in the  app or through feedback tools. 

• All surveys and focus group responses will be anonymized and aggregated for  reporting. 

• Participation in evaluation activities will be voluntary, and users may opt out at any  time. 

• Findings will be reviewed internally before being shared with Whole-Company leadership, with  summaries redacted for confidentiality. 

**7.0 FINAL EVALUATION REPORT** 

A full evaluation report will be developed following the pilot, including: 

1. • Executive summary of outcomes   
2. • Dashboard of key metrics   
3. • Thematic summaries from qualitative input   
4. • Summary of technical issues and resolutions   
5. • Recommended changes for broader rollout   
6. • Lessons learned and implications for integration with Whole-Company information & constituent management system 

This report will be presented to: 

• Center leadership 

• Whole-Company executive leadership 

• New Initiatives Team
